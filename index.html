Stegastein

Stegastein is a scenic viewpoint on road 243 in Sogn og Fjordane County, Norway. The 30m long and 4m wide platform made of steel and laminated pine offers views of Aurlandsvangen and the Aurlandsfjord

Oslo

Oslo is the capital of Norway, located on the country's southern coast at the northern end of the Oslofjord. The city is known for its parks and museums, many of which are located on the Bygde Peninsula. In Oslo, it is worth visiting the Norwegian Maritime Museum and the Viking Ship Museum, which displays wooden ships from the 9th century. The Holmenkollen ski jump, which is famous for the world's oldest ski museum, offers a beautiful view of the fjord.

Stavanger

Stavanger is a city in southwestern Norway. The 12th-century Stavanger Cathedral dominates its center. The Stavanger Museum offers insights into the city and its natural environment. The Norwegian Petroleum Museum, styled as an oil platform, features underwater installations, a large drill, and escape hatches. For shopping, head to Øvre Holmgate, a street known for its colorful buildings.

Trondheim

Trondheim is a city in central Norway, located on the shores of the Trondheim Fjord. The city is home to the 11th-century Gothic Nidaros Cathedral, famous for its ornate rose window and ornate west façade. Nearby is the Archbishop's Palace Museum, which houses archaeological finds and sculptures, including gargoyles, that once adorned the cathedral. The Ringve Museum is a musical instrument museum housed in a manor house and barn.
